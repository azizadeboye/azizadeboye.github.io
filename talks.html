Takl/Workshop 3

Spatial Modelling

Joint Modelling of Logitudinal and Time-to-Event models

Joint model (JM) of longitudinal and time-to-event outcomes is currently of a great interest in biostatistics and medical studies and highly important to observe the association effect of co-infection among the patients. In a standard JM formulation, the underlying current subject-specific value of a biomarker is assumed to associate with the risk of an event happening at the same time with an association parameter. In this study, we proposed an extension to the joint model of longitudinal-survival data, in which the longitudinal data has a cumulative and weighted effect on the hazard event function. Using a Bayesian parametric method, we propose a skewed weighted probability density function to estimate the parameters from TB/HIV dataset. The weighted cumulative effect is incorporated in calculating the hazard ratio between the subjects that provides greater flexibility for modelling the association structure of different longitudinal and survival submodel. The truncated weighted-skewed was the best fit model, which showed a maximal weight values slightly lower than weight normal density. The estimation of survival parameterization for baseline hazards indicates that it may not be an asymptotic normal distribution in some cases but with skewed distribution/heavy tail distributions.
